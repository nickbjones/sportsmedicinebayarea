The Dashboard

From the main page (sportsmedicinebayarea.org/panel), you will see a list on the left under "Pages". This is the most important part of the Panel and editing your website.

Warning: Do not click on the "Edit" or "Add" buttons here, these pages are preset and are important to the structure of the website. This may be confusing because these are also "Edit" and "Add" buttons on other pages; it is only on this page (the main Dashboard) where you should not "Edit" or "Add" any pages.

You will see the following pages in this list (feel free to use the links below if it's easier ☺):

  1. Home- This is where you will manage the content on the home page.
  2. Physicians- This is where you will manage Physician profiles.
  3. Physical Therapists- This is where you will manage Physical Therapist profiles.
  4. Patient Education- This is where you will manage Patient Education blog posts.
  5. In the News- This is where you will manage In the News blog posts.
  6. Contact- This is where you will manage faciliy contact info that is listed in the website.
  7. 404 Error- This is the error page, do not worry about this.

Managing profiles and blog posts

The following steps walk you through managing a Physician profile, but these steps also apply to Physical Therapist profiles, Patient Education posts, and In the News posts.

From the Dashboard, click Physicians which will take you to the Physicians page.

Add a new Physician profile:

  1. Create profile- At the top of the list of Physicians on the right, click Add. A window will open. Under "Add a new page", in the field that says "Title", enter the name of the Physician you wish to add, for example, "Pat Benetar". The "URL-appendix" field will auto-fill with "pat-benetar"; this will be the URL of the new Physician's page (sportsmedicinebayarea.org/physicians/pat-benetar). Click Add to create the new page.
  2. Edit info- A new page will open with several empty fields, this is the Edit page. Fill in all the desired fields, you may use an already existing page as a template. When finished, click Save at the bottom of thte page.
  3. Add photo- On the left side next to "Files", click Add. Select the photo you wish to upload. The best photos have porportions close to square or slightly taller than wide.
  4. Preview- To preview the page before publishing (it's especially good to preview the image to make sure it looks okay), under page settings on the top-left, click "Open preview". A preview of the page will open in a new tab. If it looks okay, go to the next step.
  5. Publish- When you are ready to publish this page to the live website, under page settings on the top-left, click on "Status: invisible". A window will open asking you to enter a Position; click the dropdown menu and you will see a list of numbers. This is the order in which the Physicians will appear on the Physicians page. Select in what place you want the new physician to appear in the list, otherwise select "-" and the physician will be placed at the end of the list. This order can be easily changed later. Any option selected here will enable this Physician's profile on the website. Click "Change" to publish the new profile!

Edit a Physician profile:

  1. Open profile- In the list on the left, click on the Physician's profile that you wish to edit.
  2. Edit info- Make whatever edits you wish to make (text edits, photo), then click Save at the bottom of thte page.

Remove a Physician profile:

  1. Open profile- In the list on the left, click on the Physician's profile that you wish to edit.
  2. Disable profile- Under page settings on the top-left, click on "Status: visible". In the window, click the dropdown menu and select "invisible". The profile is now disabled and cannot be viewed on the public website.

Changing the order of Physician profiles:

  1. Edit profiles- At the top of the list of Physicians on the right, click Edit.
  2. Rearrange profiles- Under "Visible pages" on the left, click and drag a profile to rearrange the order that it appears in the list of Physicians on the website.
  3. When finished, click the "Back" button on the top-left.

Formatting text

When making a profile or post, there are some guidelines you should follow in order to custom format content and also for the styling to be consistant throughout the website. This type of formatting is called "Markdown" and is pretty simple. Here are some of the most important rules:

  • Line breaks- Press enter to create a line break. If you want a new paragraph, press enter twice:
    • My first line
    My second line

    Another paragraph
  • Headlines- Add a hash "#" to the front of the text that you want to be a headline:
    • # Headline 1
    Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit.

    ## Headline 2
    Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit.

    ### Headline 3
    Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit.

    #### Headline 4
    Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit.
  • Lists- Add a dash "-" or number to the front of the text that you want to be in a list:
    • - Homer
    - Marge

    1. Bart
    2. Lisa
  • Links- The simplest way to create a link is to wrap the URL in "<" and ">":
    • <www.sportsmedicinebayarea.org>
  • Images- The simplest way to add an image is by the following:
    • (image: myawesomepicture.jpg)

This is a basic list of some of the more important Markdown rules. Read more about the formatting text here: https://getkirby.com/docs/content/text or ask me!
